Project Overview

COVID-19's effects in the U.S. can be seen in almost all industries and areas of life, from changes in the way we work and travel to how we shop. One of the most surprising , yet understandable changes that have occurred is the surge in dog adoptions.

The Washington Post reported that increased consumer demand which has led to waiting lists for certain breeds of dogs. With so much interest in adopting these four-legged-pals, our team got together to compare dog breed height/weight with dog intelligence with the hopes of helping others on selecting their future pal.

We do however, want to provide one disclaimer: Regardless of their obedience level, all dogs do have the ability to learn commands, it just may take more investment from their owner in the form of time and repetition.

Extract

Dog Intelligence: https://data.world/len/intelligence-of-dogs Breed Size: https://data.world/len/dog-canine-breed-size-akc

Transformation

Once the data was reviewed, we identified a few issues that we needed to address in the data cleaning step, such as breed name inconsistencies, and missing/invalid values.

Using Pandas, the following steps were taken to address the identified issues.

  • - The obedience rate for low degree of working/obedience intelligence category dogs was updated to reflect a numerical value of 0% to replace the imported NaN values.
  • - The obedience rate was changed into a decimal value column to facilitate SQL importation. - Dog names where replaced in both CSV files where they were inconsistent (22 entries)
  • - Dogs with no size information were dropped from the dataset. - The value counts function was used to identify height data that was not valid (such as "not found" and "na" values), which were then converted to NaN and dropped.
  • - The column titles were changed to lower case for consistency.

Once the cleaning was finalized, a postgresql connection was initiated and the cleaned tables were loaded into the database. The tables were then merged using a SQL command, and exported as a CSV file. This table may be found in the "Final Table" section of our website.
